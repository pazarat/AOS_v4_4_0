# 360-Degree Project Method

The 360 method is the universal project-engineering lens. It is not software-only and does not impose a single template.

Every high-impact assessment, plan, or execution decision checks the relevant dimensions:

- objective and scope,
- audience, beneficiaries, actors, or stakeholders,
- source of truth and authority,
- domain logic and constraints,
- workflows, scenarios, or research/content structure,
- data/evidence/content model,
- roles, permissions, safety, ethics, or governance where relevant,
- implementation or delivery path,
- quality, acceptance, validation, and tests,
- risks, failure modes, contradictions, and open questions,
- maintainability, evolution, and repair loop.

The active project decides which dimensions are applicable. The workshop supplies the scale; the project supplies the local truth.
